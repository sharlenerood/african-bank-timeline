African Bank

African Bank:
A timeline

From NAFCOC's 1964 mandate to the 2026 shareholding structure — tap on any entry to expand.

Milestone Curatorship
1964 NAFCOC inaugural meeting ▾
National African Federated Chamber of Commerce and Industry (NAFCOC) holds its inaugural meeting — the body that would later found African Bank.
1975 African Bank opens its doors ▾
Dr Sam Motsuenyane envisions a bank "for the people, by the people, serving the people." The first branch opened in Ga-Rankuwa.
1980s Services expand ▾
Expansion of services — insurance, savings accounts and credit cards are introduced.
1988 First curatorship CURATORSHIP
The bank experiences serious problems. The South African Reserve Bank (SARB) places it under curatorship for the first time.
1995 Second curatorship CURATORSHIP
African Bank is placed under curatorship for a second time, with the SARB intervening after the transition to democratic rule in 1994.
1999 Stagen acquisition ▾
African Bank completes the acquisition of Stagen.
2002 Saambou book acquired ▾
African Bank acquires the Saambou personal loan book.
2008 Ellerines Holdings acquired ▾
African Bank acquires the Ellerines Holdings group — retail furniture financing that would later prove central to the 2014 crisis.
2014 Crisis & bailout CURATORSHIP
Financial crisis and restructuring. African Bank goes into curatorship for a third time and is bailed out by a consortium led by the South African Reserve Bank and government.
2016 New African Bank opens ▾
The new African Bank opens its doors, re-established out of curatorship.
2021 'Excelerate' strategy launched ▾
African Bank embarks on a new strategy, 'Excelerate,' to reclaim its heritage and build a customer-centric, data-enabled, scalable and diversified business with a compelling listing proposition.
2022 Grindrod & Ubank acquired ▾
African Bank acquires Grindrod Bank and the assets and liabilities of Ubank.
2023 Integration steps up ▾
Grindrod and Ubank integration continues, stepping up 'Excelerate' initiatives to drive growth.
2024 Section 54 approval ▾
Section 54 approval and the full divisionalisation of Grindrod Bank into African Bank, and the birth of an employee share ownership scheme.
2025 Consolidation ▾
African Bank consolidates new capabilities and positions the group for growth.
SOURCE: AFRICAN BANK INTEGRATED REPORT 2025
